# Meet the Team: Testing

This page applies the testing strategies from [Testing](/tutorial/testing) and [Screen Reader Testing](/tutorial/testing/screen-reader-testing) to the Maple Valley Health Meet the Team page.

## Keyboard Testing Walkthrough

Put your mouse aside and work through these tasks:

**1. Navigate to the filter:**

- Tab to the specialty filter dropdown
- Is the filter labeled? Can you select a specialty with arrow keys?
- After selecting, does the result count update? Can you hear or see the change?

**2. Browse the doctor list:**

- Tab through the doctor entries. Is the order logical (name → edit → delete → next doctor)?
- Can you reach every edit and delete button?
- Is the focus indicator visible on every element?

**3. Delete a doctor:**

- Tab to a delete button and press Enter
- Where does focus go? Is it on the next doctor, or did it disappear?
- If you deleted the last doctor, does focus land on a reasonable element?

**4. Open the add/edit form:**

- Tab to the "Add new doctor" button and press Enter
- Can you Tab through every form field?
- Submit the form empty — are errors announced? Does focus move to the first error?
- Can you dismiss the form with Escape?

**5. Use the filter at 200% zoom:**

- Zoom to 200% and repeat the filter interaction
- Does the layout reflow? Can you still read the doctor names and specialties?

## Screen Reader Testing Walkthrough

Turn on your screen reader (review how to [turn it off first](/tutorial/testing/screen-reader-testing#before-you-start-know-how-to-turn-it-off)) and test:

**1. Page structure:**

- Navigate by heading. Do you hear each doctor's name as an h3?
- Is the page title announced as h1?
- Can you get a quick overview of the page by scanning headings alone?

**2. Doctor information:**

- Navigate to a doctor entry. Is the name, specialty, and contact info announced in a logical order?
- Is the photo described with the doctor's name and specialty, or is it silent/unhelpful?

**3. Interactive elements:**

- Navigate to the edit and delete buttons. Are they announced as "Edit Dr. Maria Reyes" and "Delete Dr. Maria Reyes" — or just "Edit" and "Delete"?
- Activate delete. What does the screen reader announce? Is the deletion confirmed?

**4. Filter:**

- Navigate to the specialty filter. Is it announced with its label?
- Change the filter. Is the updated result count announced automatically (via `aria-live`)?

**5. Form:**

- Open the add doctor form. Are all fields announced with their labels?
- Which fields are announced as required?
- Submit with errors. Are the error messages announced? Can you navigate to the field with the error?

## What Jordan Found

When Jordan tested the rebuilt Meet the Team page, she documented:

**Passed:**

- All doctor names are scannable as h3 headings
- Filter is labeled and result count updates are announced
- Edit/delete buttons include the doctor's name
- Form fields are labeled and errors are linked with `aria-describedby`
- Layout reflows cleanly at 200% and 400% zoom

**Issues found:**

- After deleting a doctor, focus moved correctly to the next doctor — but there was no announcement confirming the deletion. Added `role="status"` to the confirmation message.
- The specialty filter dropdown didn't announce the number of options available. Added `aria-describedby` pointing to a count.
- At 400% zoom, the delete confirmation dialog's "Cancel" button was partially off-screen. Adjusted the dialog's max-width.

None of these issues were caught by automated tools. All of them would have affected the users from the research study.

## Using AI to Plan Test Scenarios

AI assistants can help you think through what to test:

- **"What keyboard testing scenarios should I cover for a filterable list with add, edit, and delete?"** — generates a testing checklist
- **"A screen reader user reported that deleting an item leaves focus in a confusing state. What should I test?"** — helps you systematically reproduce and verify the fix
- **"What are the WCAG success criteria relevant to a form with required fields and inline validation?"** — maps your feature to specific criteria to test against

AI is particularly useful for generating the testing plan. The actual testing must be done by a human with real assistive technology.
