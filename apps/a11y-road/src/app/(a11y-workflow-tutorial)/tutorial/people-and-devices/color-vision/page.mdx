# Color Vision Differences

Color blindness affects roughly 8% of men and 0.5% of women of Northern European descent, with varying rates across populations. The most common form is red-green color blindness, but there are several types that affect how people distinguish colors.

**Who:** People with protanopia, deuteranopia, tritanopia, or other forms of color vision deficiency. Also people using monochrome displays or e-ink readers.

**What they use:**

- Most people with color vision differences don't use specialized software — they rely on the design itself being clear without depending on color alone
- **Color filters** built into operating systems (Windows, macOS, iOS)
- **Browser extensions** that simulate different types of color blindness (useful for designers and developers during testing)

**What they rely on:** Color never being the *only* way to convey information. Error states that use icons or text, not just red. Charts that use patterns or labels, not just color coding. Required fields marked with text or symbols, not just a color change. Sufficient contrast between adjacent colors.

**Quick check:** *If I remove color, does the meaning remain clear?*
